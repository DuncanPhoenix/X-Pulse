Motivation

Modern 4th-generation X-ray facilities provide unprecedented opportunities for probing ultrafast dynamics. Their exceptionally high photon flux per pulse (109 - 1012 photons per pulse), combined with ultra-short pulse duration (~10 fs and ~100 ps at XFEL’s and synchrotrons) with pulse megahertz repetition rates, enables ultra-fast single-shot/single exposure imaging of stochastic phenomena in matter.

Funding and Project Synergies


This project builds on and complements several national international and European initiatives:


1. Röntgen Ångström Cluster, InVision, 2019 - 2024. PI’s: John Banhard TU Berlin, Stephen Hall, Lund University, Patrik Vagovic work package leader at EuXFEL.

www.rontgen-angstrom.eu


2. European XFEL R&D project: MHz X-ray microscopy (2020–2022). PI: Patrik Vagovic


3. HORIZON-EIC Pathfinder (2022–2025), Grant No. 101046448. PI: Patrik Vagovic

https://eic.ec.europa.eu/eic-funding-opportunities/eic-pathfinder_en


4. Slovak Technological and Innovation Consortium project, “Stephanik” Project (2025– 2027).


5. STFC International pipeline project (application submitted) to support X-PULSE activities at European XFEL.

https://www.xfel.eu/


Access and funding at P25 will leverage these investments, creating a platform with high international visibility and strong user engagement. We are preparing to propose a user consortium to the European XFEL management, where our instruments will be installed at SASE1, and a collaboration with ESRF ID19 on fast imaging is already established. The fastimaging capabilities at P25 will significantly enhance Europe’s existing strengths in this rapidly growing field by expanding access to advanced fast and ultrafast X-ray imaging.
